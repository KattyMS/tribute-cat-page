Our Mysterious Companion

Cats have walked beside us for thousands of years—quietly, gracefully, and often on their own terms. From ancient temples to cozy modern homes, they have captured our imagination and love. This page is a tribute to the cat: a creature of elegance, mystery, and affection.

Cats in History

In Ancient Egypt, cats were sacred. They were symbols of protection and were even mummified like pharaohs. In Japan, cats have long been considered good luck—especially the iconic maneki-neko, the beckoning cat often seen in shops. In Europe, they were both feared and cherished, associated with both witchcraft and royalty.

Fascinating Cat Facts

  • Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
  • Their purring may help heal bones and reduce stress.
  • They sleep an average of 12-16 hours a day.
  • Cats "talk" with their tails and eyes, not just their meows.

Cats in Pop Culture

From Garfield and Hello Kitty to Salem the cat in "Sabrina" and the elegant Duchess in Disney's "The Aristocats", cats have found their way into books, movies, and even politics (remember Socks, the White House cat). Most recently, the Oscar-winning animated feature Flow (2024), a dialogue-free story about a resilient cat navigating a flood with other animals, captivated audiences worldwide and received the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature at the 97th Oscars—a historic first for Latvia.

Why We Love Cats

Is it their independence? Their silent companionship? The way they curl up beside us or chase dust in the air? Cats bring a quiet joy to our lives, teaching us to observe, to rest, and to enjoy simple moments.
